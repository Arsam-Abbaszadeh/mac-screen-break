# Screen Break

`Screen Break` is a macOS app for planned interruption.

It solves a simple problem: sometimes you want to let yourself do something distracting for a limited amount of time, but when that time is up, you want the break to be enforced.

Example:

- I want to browse, scroll, search, or watch something for 20 minutes.
- After that, I want the app to force me to stop.
- I want a break period where I cannot easily continue using the distracting application.

This app gives you two timers:

1. A timer for when the screen lockdown starts
2. A timer for how long the screen lockdown lasts

When the start timer finishes, the app places an overlay across your screens and makes it inconvenient to keep going. The goal is to interrupt momentum and create a real break instead of relying on willpower in the moment.

This is useful for planned exposure to dopaminergic activities: you decide in advance that you are going to engage for a limited amount of time, and you also decide in advance that the stop point will be enforced.

The point is not perfect device lockdown. The point is to add enough friction that continuing mindlessly becomes harder than stopping.

## Why I made it

I made this because I wanted a way to limit time spent on dopaminergic activities on my Mac without relying on self-control at the exact moment I was supposed to stop.

I wanted something that let me say:

`I want to do this for 20 minutes, and then I want to forcefully take a break.`
